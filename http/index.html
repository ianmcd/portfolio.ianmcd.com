ShoreTel (2016–Current)

In early 2016, Corvisa was acquired by ShoreTel. Having never experienced an acquisition or design in a corporate setting, I immediately found myself operating outside of my comfort zone. This ended up being an invaluable period of growth for me as I took on more responsibility and helped lead an effort to update ShoreTel’s identity and eliminate out-of-date design practices.

There were plenty of challenges along the way, and I was admittedly not so great at navigating the resistance set before me initially. I found that my sometimes-overwhelming passion for design needed to tempered with a bit of patience and persistence if I were going to be a successful. Once I learned these critical lessons I found myself influencing quite a bit of change in a relatively short period of time.

In my time here I was an active member of the Global Corporate Marketing team and was involved in a variety initiatives and lead several projects of my own. In my first year ShoreTel under went a substantial brand update and reassessment of design strategies. In 2017 we launched a major redesign of our primary marketing website as well as laying the groundwork for the next few phases of improvement.

Corvisa (2013–2016)

I joined the Corvisa team in 2013. Initially working in a team of two, I performed a wide variety of design related tasks. Over my first year the company grew quickly and as more structured design roles were assigned I took on the responsibility of designing, developing & maintaining marketing related web entities.

As a member of the marketing team, I learned a lot about designing within a team and came to value focusing and improving upon design within a single brand. I also found myself enjoying the more strategic thinking involved with web marketing—such as creating user flows, A/B testing and making data-driven decisions.

I found a lot of value in the camaraderie and shared responsibility that being in a team brought. I was also graced with the good fortune to work under a leader who allowed me the freedom to push myself, both professionally and creatively, and make mistakes—trusting that ultimately I would not only learn from those mistakes but be better because of them.

Trivera Interactive (2008–2013)

I was thrilled when I was hired at Trivera. Getting to learn design and development at a respected Milwaukee agency was an exciting prospect. It was here that I truly fell in love with the world of web design—embracing the culture and developing a never-ending desire to learn.

I quickly developed a passion for design and development. Having the opportunity to work on multiple—and sometimes very different—projects was valuable in helping me find myself as a designer and an individual. I spent several years with Trivera learning the ins and outs of web design and development.

While I’ve ultimately determined agency life is not for me, I do appreciate the value in the variety and passion that an agency brings. At Trivera I identified my style, learned how to deal with a variety of clients and made personal connections that will stay with me for the rest of my life. It was an experience I’ve come to value deeply.
